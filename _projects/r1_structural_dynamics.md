My primary research focus is on the dynamic behaviour of large-scale reinforced concrete structures, particularly hyperbolic cooling towers, under seismic excitation. Using the commercial finite element software **ABAQUS**, I develop detailed numerical models to investigate structural response, energy dissipation, and failure mechanisms.

### Key Contributions

- Developed finite element models of the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant cooling tower (175 m height) using both **Embedded Reinforcement** and **Simplified Timoshenko Beam** column approaches.
- Conducted **non-linear time history analysis** using the 2001 Bhuj/Kachchh earthquake record.
- Compared the two modelling approaches in terms of natural frequency, displacement, meridional stress, hoop stress, and base shear.
- Demonstrated that the Embedded Reinforcement Column model captures plastic hinge formation and crack propagation more realistically.

### Relevance

Bangladesh is located in a seismically active region. The proximity of the **Madhupur Fault** to the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant makes rigorous seismic analysis of this infrastructure critically important.
