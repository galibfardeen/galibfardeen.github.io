The Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant is Bangladesh's first nuclear power plant, located near the Madhupur Fault — a seismically significant zone. Ensuring the structural integrity of its critical infrastructure is of national importance.

### Research Focus

- Finite element modelling of the **hyperbolic cooling tower** at Rooppur NPP, with a height of 175 m, supported by 100 Λ-shaped RCC columns.
- Assessment of seismic vulnerability under realistic ground motion records.
- Evaluation of wind loading effects on the thin-shell tower structure.
- Comparison of column modelling strategies and their influence on predicted structural response.

### Conference Presentation

- Ahmad Chy, I.U., **Fardin, M.A.G.**, Awall, M.R. and Biswas, A. (2024). *Dynamic Analysis of the Hyperbolic Cooling Tower at Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant.* **7th International Conference on Advances in Civil Engineering (ICACE-2024)**, CUET, Chittagong, Bangladesh. Paper ID: 495, 12–14 December 2024.

### Significance

Bangladesh's historical seismic record and tectonic setting indicate that a major earthquake is overdue. This research contributes to evidence-based safety assessment frameworks for critical national infrastructure.
