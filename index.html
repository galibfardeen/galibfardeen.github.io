research

My primary research investigates the dynamic behaviour of large-scale hyperbolic cooling towers under seismic and wind loading, with a focus on the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant in Bangladesh — the country's first nuclear power plant, located near the seismically active Madhupur Fault.

Using ABAQUS, I developed detailed finite element models of the 175 m cooling tower and compared two column modelling strategies: an Embedded Reinforcement approach and a Simplified Timoshenko Beam approach. Both models were subjected to the 2001 Bhuj/Kachchh earthquake and wind loads calculated via Greiner's equation per BNBC-2020.

Stress analysis of the Rooppur NPP cooling tower under dynamic loading — comparing Embedded Reinforcement and Simplified Column modelling approaches.

Ongoing work includes CFRP-strengthened beam-column connections under monotonic and cyclic loading (in submission, 2025) and impact behaviour of pressurised uPVC pipes (in preparation, 2025).

publications

  1. J. Build. Rehabil.
    Dynamic analysis of cooling tower considering two different column modeling approaches
    Intekhab Uddin Ahmad Chy, Md Asadullahil Galib Fardin, Md Robiul Awall, and 1 more author
    Journal of Building Pathology and Rehabilitation, 2025
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    Nuclear power plants use cooling towers, which are frequently hyperboloid in shape, to disperse excess heat created during power generation. In this study, two models of a hyperbolic natural draught cooling tower were made — one with Embedded Reinforcement Columns and another with Simplified Columns modelled as Timoshenko Beam-type members. Both models were exposed to seismic and wind excitations and their responses were compared. The Embedded Reinforcement Column model exhibited greater flexibility and reduced stiffness, resulting in lower natural frequencies and larger displacements under both loads. The findings are relevant given Bangladesh’s increased seismic susceptibility near the Madhupur Fault and the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant.
