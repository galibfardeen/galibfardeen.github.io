Hydrodynamic Changes of Estuarine Islands in the Meghna River

EGU General Assembly 2026

This study analyses long-term hydrodynamic changes across seven estuarine islands in the Meghna Estuary under future climate scenarios, using Delft3D simulations for 2000, 2024, and 2050.

Future discharge was generated from MIROC6 projections under SSP2-4.5 and SSP5-8.5, bias-corrected and routed through HEC-HMS. High tides near Char Kukri Mukri are projected to rise by 30–35 cm and water depths around Hatiya and Manipura by 0.5–0.8 m by 2050.

Presented — EGU General Assembly 2026

Post-Flood Sediment Deposition Analysis Using SAR Interferometry

EGU General Assembly 2026

This study quantifies post-flood sediment deposition in the Feni floodplain, Bangladesh, following the catastrophic August 2024 flood triggered by overflow from the Dumboor dam in Tripura state.

SAR Interferometry using Sentinel-1 SLC products was applied in SNAP to generate Digital Elevation Models before and after the flood. Eight cross-section profiles in HEC-RAS revealed an average sediment deposition of 2.7 cm and a maximum of 7.5 cm across the region.

Structural Dynamics of Cooling Towers

Journal of Building Pathology and Rehabilitation, Springer

My primary research investigates the dynamic behaviour of large-scale hyperbolic cooling towers under seismic and wind loading, with a focus on the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant, Bangladesh's first nuclear power plant, situated near the seismically active Madhupur Fault.

Using ABAQUS, I compared two column modelling strategies, Embedded Reinforcement and Simplified Timoshenko Beam, subjecting both to the 2001 Bhuj/Kachchh earthquake (non-linear time history) and wind loads via Greiner's equation (BNBC-2020).

Published — Journal of Building Pathology and Rehabilitation, Springer, 2025

Impact Behaviour of Pressurised uPVC Pipes

This study examines the low-velocity lateral impact response of water-pressurised uPVC pipes under both-ends-clamped and one-clamped-one-roller support conditions, across four drop heights (1.0–2.75 m), using a locally built ESP32-based pressure and velocity measuring unit.

Clamped supports yielded up to 6.34% higher peak contact force. Internal pressure reached 3063 kPa at 2.75 m drop. FEA matched experiment within ~14%.

CFRP-Strengthened Beam-Column Steel Joints

This study investigates mild steel beam-column T-joints retrofitted with Carbon Fibre Reinforced Polymer (CFRP) under monotonic and cyclic loading, using ABAQUS FEA validated against experimental data.

Three profiles, Square Hollow, I-Section, and Circular Hollow, were modelled with and without CFRP. Retrofitting improved load capacity by up to 26.71% and energy dissipation by up to 33.20% under cyclic loading.

  3. J. Build. Rehabil.
    Dynamic analysis of cooling tower considering two different column modeling approaches
    Intekhab Uddin Ahmad Chy, Md Asadullahil Galib Fardin, Md Robiul Awall, and Anik Biswas
    Journal of Building Pathology and Rehabilitation, 2025
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    Nuclear power plants use cooling towers, which are frequently hyperboloid in shape, to disperse excess heat created during power generation. In this study, two models of a hyperbolic natural draught cooling tower were made — one with Embedded Reinforcement Columns and another with Simplified Columns modelled as Timoshenko Beam-type members. Both models were exposed to seismic and wind excitations and their responses were compared. The Embedded Reinforcement Column model exhibited greater flexibility and reduced stiffness, resulting in lower natural frequencies and larger displacements under both loads. The findings are relevant given Bangladesh’s increased seismic susceptibility near the Madhupur Fault and the Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant.

  4. Adv. Civil Eng.
    Impact Behavior of Pressurized uPVC Pipe: An Experimental and Numerical Analysis
    M. R. Afroz, Md Asadullahil Galib Fardin, T. U. Ahmed, J. B. Bashar, H. Ahmed, and 2 more authors
    Advances in Civil Engineering, 2025
    Submitted
    Abs Bib

    This study experimentally and numerically investigates the low-velocity lateral impact response of water-pressurised uPVC pipes under two support conditions — both-ends-clamped and one-clamped-one-roller — at four drop heights. A locally developed ESP32-based pressure and velocity measuring unit recorded internal pressure fluctuations in real time. Clamped supports produced up to 6.34% higher peak contact force. Internal pressure spikes reached 3063 kPa and grew with impact energy. Plastic deformation zones expanded radially with drop height. ABAQUS FEA results matched experimental data within approximately 14%.

  5. Adv. Civil Eng.
    Numerical Investigation on Behavior of CFRP Strengthened Beam-Column Steel Joint under Compressive and Cyclic Loading
    Kazi Rashadul Islam and Md Asadullahil Galib Fardin
    Advances in Civil Engineering, 2025
    Under Review
    Abs Bib

    This study investigates the behavior of CFRP-strengthened mild steel beam-column T-joints under monotonic and cyclic loading using ABAQUS finite element analysis. Three section profiles (Square, I-section, Circular Hollow) were modelled with and without CFRP retrofitting. CFRP strengthening improved load-carrying capacity by up to 26.71% under monotonic loading and enhanced energy dissipation by up to 33.20% under cyclic loading, demonstrating significant seismic performance gains.
